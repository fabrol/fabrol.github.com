I grew up in Delhi, went to a small liberal arts school in New Jersey, and have spent the last decade living between San Francisco and Brooklyn, both of which I love dearly.

I care about reducing inequity in access to essential needs and increasing human agency over our lives.

I thrive at the intersection of user problems and technical complexity. I love building systems, taxonomies and narratives that bridge complexity and make things useful to people.

I started out doing field work at UNICEF in Madagascar, building remote sensing systems for health monitoring.

Quickly, I felt a pull to learn more and ended up joining a small startup called Pure Storage, where I ended up staying for 6 years. From early days through IPO, i got a chance to build at different scales, from hands on building distributed databases with 99.9999% uptime, to leading an ML SaaS optimization tool from inception through industry leadership.

Once I had my green card, I took my learnings on how to build, and tried to align *what* i build more closely with my values.

It was mid pandemic, and I found an opportunity to lead the Public Health teams at Meta. It was a whirlwind working with governments all over the world, across FB, IG and WA; and deep algorithmic tech, to reach our goal of connecting 50M people with the vaccine. It made me learn reflect deeply on the role of algorithms in public life.

After Meta, I took a sabbatical, the first since undergrad, to taste freedom post-green card. I spent time writing op-eds on AI and social media policy, exploring how technology can, and should, serve society in fairer ways.

It’s an exploration I’m still on, especially in my current work around increasing user agency over their data and how AI can support critical needs like healthcare, communication, and access to information.
